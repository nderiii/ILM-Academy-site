Admission Process

Our straightforward process to join the ILM Academy family

1

Inquiry & School Tour

Submit an inquiry form or call our admissions office to schedule a personalized tour of our facilities.

Tours available Monday-Friday, 9am-2pm
2

Application Submission

Complete the online application form and submit required documents including academic records and birth certificate.

3

Assessment & Interview

Students complete grade-level assessments while parents meet with administration to discuss educational goals.

Assessments scheduled within 1 week of application
4

Admission Decision

Receive notification of acceptance within 5 business days with enrollment instructions.

Decisions communicated via email and phone
5

Enrollment Completion

Submit enrollment forms, health records, and tuition deposit to secure your child's place.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about our admissions process

What are the school hours?

Our regular school hours are 8:00 AM to 3:30 PM for all grades. Preschool has a half-day option ending at 12:30 PM. Extended care is available until 6:00 PM for an additional fee.

Is there a uniform requirement?

Yes, ILM Academy has a modest uniform policy. Our uniforms consist of navy blue or khaki pants/skirts with white or light blue polo shirts. Complete uniform details and vendors are provided upon enrollment.

Do you offer transportation services?

Yes, we offer optional bus transportation serving most areas in the city. Our buses are equipped with seat belts and supervised by monitors.

What is your teacher-to-student ratio?

We maintain small class sizes to ensure personalized attention. Our average ratios are:

  • Preschool: 1:8 with teacher and assistant
  • Elementary: 1:12
  • Middle/High School: 1:15

How do you handle students with learning differences?

We provide limited accommodations for students with mild learning differences. Our learning specialist works with teachers to implement appropriate strategies. However, we are not equipped to serve students with significant special needs. We recommend discussing your child's specific needs during the admissions process.
